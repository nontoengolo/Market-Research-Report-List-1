<p><p>The systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) drug market comprises various treatment types, each targeting different aspects of the disease. Corticosteroids and NSAIDs manage inflammation and pain. Anti-inflammatories reduce tissue inflammation. Disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drugs (DMARDs) slow disease progression. Antimalarials are used for skin and joint symptoms. BLyS-specific inhibitors and monoclonal antibodies target specific immune pathways. Immunosuppressive agents modulate the immune response, while anticoagulants prevent blood clots, addressing the increased thrombotic risk in SLE patients. Together, these drugs provide comprehensive care for SLE.</p></p>
