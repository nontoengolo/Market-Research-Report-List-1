<p><p>The contraceptive market comprises two primary types: contraceptive drugs and contraceptive devices. Contraceptive drugs include hormonal options like pills, patches, and injections that prevent ovulation. They are often favored for their convenience and effectiveness. On the other hand, contraceptive devices encompass barrier methods such as condoms, intrauterine devices (IUDs), and implants that physically prevent sperm from reaching the egg or create an unfavorable environment for fertilization. Both segments cater to diverse consumer needs and preferences for effective family planning.</p></p>
